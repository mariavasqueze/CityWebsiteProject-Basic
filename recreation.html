Shopping

Madrid is very well known for shopping and it has stores of all categories and prices.

Madrid's most popular shopping areas are:
  • Calle Gran Vía
  • Calle de Preciados
  • Calle de Serrano
  • Calle the Fuencarral
  • El Rastro on Calle de la Ribera de Curtidores

Sightseeing

Madrid is an extremly walkable and safe city. You can walk through the main plazas and parks and take the metro all around the city. You can go from La Puerta del Sol in the city center to Plaza Mayor, and then take the guided tour at The Royal Palace. If you prefer parks, go to El Retiro park where you can take a bike or a boat in the lake, and visit Madrid's Crystal Palace hidden in the woods. Finally, walk to Serrano street to find great restaurants and stores.

Tapas and Wine

Madrid is one of Europe's culinary capitals and Tapas are those bite-sized morsels that serve as the great samplers of Spanish cuisine. It's most common to get tapas at a restaurant that a big meal. Tapas bars are all around the city with a variety of flavours you can't even imagine. The best combination is to have tapas with an excelent wine, and what better than spanish wine. It's a delicious inexpensive way to get to know Madrid's gastronomy.

Incredible Views

With just a few skylines, the views of Madrid can go for miles from any high point. You can go to see the Gran Vía from above for free at the top floor of the Callaro El Corte Inglés If you are a soccer fan, you can have a great time visiting the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium, and if sunsets are your thing, watch the most colorful sunsets at Templo de Debod, which is an Egyptian temple that looks overs the hill.
